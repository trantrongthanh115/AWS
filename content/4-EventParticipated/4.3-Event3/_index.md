# Event Report: FCAJ Community Day 2026

### General Information

* **Event Title:** FCAJ Community Day 2026
* **Date & Time:** Saturday, June 13, 2026
* **Venue:** Bitexco Financial Tower, Ho Chi Minh City
* **My Role:** Attendee

---

### Overview & Objectives

The FCAJ Community Day in June was designed to connect students with the realities of the IT industry. The sessions focused on Cloud Computing, DevOps practices, System Design, and professional career development. In addition to technical insights, the speakers offered valuable advice on corporate culture, building a growth mindset, and developing the essential skills required to work in global technology companies.

By listening to experienced Cloud and Data engineers, attendees learned how AWS services power real-world applications and gained clarity on how to prepare for long-term career success.

---

### Session Breakdown & Insights

#### 1. Designing a Scalable URL Shortener on AWS
* **Speakers:** Anh Kien & Anh Tho

This presentation broke down the architectural challenges of designing a production-grade URL shortening service. A successful URL shortener must handle more than just string manipulation; it requires high availability, low latency, fault tolerance, and the ability to scale seamlessly under heavy concurrent traffic.

* **Key Points Discussed:**
  * End-to-end request routing and redirect mechanisms.
  * Selecting the right AWS services to handle storage, caching, and redirection.
  * Strategies for database scaling and performance optimization.

* **My Takeaway:** The session emphasized system design thinking. Building real-world services requires looking beyond basic functional code to evaluate architecture, database choices, caching layers, and load distribution together.

---

#### 2. Demystifying the DevOps Role
* **Speaker:** Anh Truong Huu Trong

This session addressed a common misconception: that DevOps is solely about building CI/CD pipelines or managing servers in the cloud. In practice, a DevOps Engineer serves as a vital bridge between software development and IT operations to ensure stable, automated, and secure software delivery.

* **Core Skills Highlighted:**
  * Strong foundations in Linux administration, networking, and Git.
  * Mastery of containerization technologies (such as Docker) and cloud platforms.
  * Soft skills including cross-team communication, proactive problem-solving, and automation.

* **My Takeaway:** I was particularly inspired by the speaker's advice: *"Tools change, fundamentals stay."* It is a strong reminder to master core system administration and software engineering principles before trying to learn every new tool in the ecosystem.

---

#### 3. My Path from First Cloud AI Journey to an AWS Partner
* **Speaker:** Anh Hoang Hieu Nghi

In this final talk, the speaker shared their personal career progression starting as a student in the First Cloud AI Journey program to eventually working directly with AWS partner organizations. The discussion highlighted key milestones, recommended certifications, and the value of hands-on projects in bridging the gap between university coursework and corporate expectations.

* **My Takeaway:** This session motivated me to focus on building practical side projects and pursuing relevant AWS certifications to open up exciting career opportunities within the cloud computing ecosystem.
